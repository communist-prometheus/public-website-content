## The Problem

Quality education and knowledge remain locked behind paywalls, expensive institutions, and geographic barriers. The commodification of information creates a two-tier society where only the privileged have access to the tools for advancement.

## Our Position

We believe that **knowledge is a commons** — it belongs to all of humanity and should be freely shared. This means:

- **Open access publishing**: Research and educational materials should be freely available
- **Community-driven education**: Learning should be collaborative, not competitive
- **Multilingual resources**: Knowledge should be accessible in every language
- **No paywalls**: Critical information should never be locked behind a paywall

## What We Propose

1. Create and curate free educational resources across disciplines
2. Support open-access publishing initiatives
3. Translate important works into underserved languages
4. Build platforms that prioritize knowledge sharing over profit
